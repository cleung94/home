My Journey on Genshin Impact

Exploring the world, characters, and stories that keep me returning to Genshin Impact.

April 2026 10 min read

Out of all the games I have played throughout my life, Genshin Impact is easily my favorite. I started playing the game when it first released, and ever since then it has become something I return to almost every single day. What originally began as curiosity quickly turned into a long-term journey through a world that feels incredibly immersive and alive.

When Genshin Impact first launched, I remember simply wanting to try it out because of how visually impressive it looked. The art style immediately stood out to me. The colorful landscapes, the anime-inspired characters, and the fantasy setting all made the game feel unique compared to many other RPGs at the time. I expected to play for a little while and move on like many other games, but that never happened.

Instead, the world of Teyvat slowly became something I genuinely grew attached to. The more I explored, the more I realized how much care and attention the developers had put into every corner of the game.

Genshin Impact isn't just a game to me — it feels like a world that constantly invites you to slow down, explore, and appreciate the journey.

A World That Feels Alive

One of the things that immediately drew me into Genshin Impact was the environment design. The world is filled with beautiful landscapes that feel handcrafted and intentional. Mountains, forests, cities, ruins, and hidden caves are scattered across the map, and each region carries its own identity.

Mondstadt feels peaceful and open, inspired by European landscapes and wind-swept plains. Liyue feels rich with culture and history, filled with towering mountains and lantern-lit harbors. Inazuma introduces a darker, more mysterious atmosphere with its stormy islands and tense political backdrop.

Each region not only looks different but also feels different emotionally. The music, architecture, and storytelling change depending on where you are in the world. It creates the feeling that you are traveling across entirely different nations rather than simply moving between levels in a game.

What makes exploration even more enjoyable are the countless small details hidden throughout the environment. There are puzzles tucked into cliffsides, secret chests hidden behind waterfalls, and small environmental clues that hint at stories that happened long before the player arrived.

Sometimes I find myself wandering the map with no particular goal in mind, simply enjoying the atmosphere. Few games create that sense of calm exploration the way Genshin Impact does.

Storytelling That Slowly Unfolds

Another reason I have continued playing the game for so long is the storytelling. Genshin Impact introduces a large cast of characters, each with their own motivations, histories, and personal struggles. Instead of presenting everything at once, the game slowly reveals pieces of each character's story over time.

This slow approach to storytelling makes the world feel deeper. Every quest adds a little more context to the characters and the history of the world. Sometimes a small detail mentioned in one story might suddenly become important much later.

Beyond the main storyline, there are also countless smaller stories hidden in side quests and character interactions. Many of these stories are surprisingly emotional and meaningful. Some explore themes of loss, responsibility, hope, and perseverance.

What makes these stories stand out is that they often feel wholesome and sincere. The characters interact with each other in ways that feel natural, humorous, and sometimes heartfelt. These moments give the world a sense of warmth that keeps players invested in the journey.

The Characters

The characters themselves are another reason the game has remained so enjoyable. Each character has their own personality, combat style, and background story that adds depth to the world.

Rather than feeling like simple playable avatars, the characters feel like individuals with unique identities. Some are serious and responsible, others are playful or mischievous, and some carry emotional stories that slowly unfold through their character quests.

Because of this, building teams in Genshin Impact feels more personal. You're not just choosing abilities that work well together — you're also choosing characters you enjoy seeing interact with the world.

This character-driven storytelling helps make the world feel alive.

The Gameplay Experience

The combat system in Genshin Impact is built around elemental reactions. Each character represents a different elemental power, such as Pyro, Hydro, Electro, Cryo, or Anemo. By combining these elements in combat, players can trigger powerful reactions that deal additional damage or create unique effects.

This system encourages experimentation. Players can mix and match different characters to create team combinations that work well together. Switching characters mid-battle to trigger reactions adds a satisfying rhythm to the gameplay.

While the combat is enjoyable, I do think it could become more challenging in the future. Compared to some newer action RPGs, Genshin Impact's battles can feel slightly simpler once you fully understand the mechanics.

Games like Wuthering Waves and Arknights Endfield appear to be pushing combat systems further with faster reaction-based gameplay and more complex enemy behaviors. Those developments are exciting to watch, and I sometimes wish Genshin Impact would evolve its combat in similar ways.

If I could change one thing about Genshin Impact, it would probably be introducing more dynamic combat encounters. I would love to see something closer to the fluid, high-energy combat style of Zenless Zone Zero incorporated into Genshin's world.

That kind of real-time intensity combined with Genshin Impact's exploration would make the experience even more engaging.

The Value of Time Spent

Like many players who have spent years with the game, I have occasionally spent money on it. Genshin Impact is technically a free-to-play game, but it offers optional purchases for new characters and weapons through its gacha system.

For me, those purchases felt reasonable considering how many hours I have spent playing the game. When a game provides hundreds of hours of entertainment, supporting it occasionally doesn't feel unreasonable.

The amount of content the developers continue to release is impressive. New regions, new characters, and new stories continue to expand the world over time.

Few games manage to maintain that level of long-term engagement with their player base.

My Favorite Character: Hu Tao

Out of all the characters in Genshin Impact, my favorite is probably Hu Tao.

At first glance, Hu Tao appears playful, mischievous, and somewhat unpredictable. She jokes frequently, teases the people around her, and often approaches serious topics with humor.

However, once you learn more about her story, you begin to see a deeper side to her personality.

Hu Tao serves as the director of the Wangsheng Funeral Parlor, a role that requires her to confront the concept of life and death regularly. Despite carrying such a heavy responsibility, she maintains an optimistic and determined attitude toward life.

What makes her character interesting is the balance between humor and emotional strength. She refuses to let sadness define her perspective, choosing instead to move forward with confidence and determination.

That strength is something I really admire.

Even with the challenges she faces, she continues pushing forward and fulfilling her responsibilities. She doesn't allow her past to hold her back or weaken her resolve.

At the same time, her playful personality creates many comedic moments in the game. Her habit of joking about funeral services and offering playful discounts to customers adds a unique sense of humor that makes her stand out among the characters.

The contrast between her responsibilities and her playful attitude is part of what makes her so memorable.

Why I Keep Coming Back

After all these years, the reason I keep returning to Genshin Impact is simple. The game feels comforting.

It is a place where I can explore, relax, and experience stories at my own pace. Whether I am discovering a new region, completing quests, or simply wandering through the world while listening to the music, the experience always feels enjoyable.

After long days of work or stressful moments in life, having a world like that to return to is something I truly appreciate.

For me, Genshin Impact is more than just a game. It is a journey that I have been on for years, and a world that I have grown attached to over time.

Even as new games release and the gaming industry continues evolving, Genshin Impact remains one of the few games that I consistently return to.

And honestly, I don't see that changing anytime soon.
