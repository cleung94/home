Quick Summary

I was genuinely excited that The Devil Wears Prada finally received a sequel. The first movie was already such a wow-factor film that it almost felt risky to come back to it after so many years. Usually, when a movie performs that well and earns an “Absolutely” rewatch value from fans, a second or third movie can struggle to live up to the original.

For me, The Devil Wears Prada 2 was more than enough to justify its return. It felt like a stylish, funny, and satisfying theater watch. I found myself laughing with the audience during many of the iconic lines, and by the time I left, I felt happy that the movie existed.

One note for anyone watching: there is a short after-credit scene, so be sure to wait until the end of the credits.

Story & Writing

6.5 / 10
The movie gives fans familiar moments, callbacks, and parallels to the original
Some of the story choices felt more like references than a natural continuation
The romance felt too sudden and needed more development, conflict, and emotional payoff

The story and writing were the weakest parts of the movie for me. I enjoyed the nostalgia, but I also felt like the plot leaned too heavily on parallels from the first film. Instead of fully bridging the gap between the original story and this new chapter, some moments felt abrupt.

I wanted the plot to have more depth and freshness. The movie had plenty of familiar energy, but it sometimes felt like it was trying to remind us why we loved the first movie instead of fully giving us a new story that could stand on its own.

The romance also moved too quickly. It felt like it was simply handed to the audience without enough trial, tribulation, or emotional build-up. I wanted Andy to receive a more satisfying story arc, but instead it felt like she was suddenly given a supportive man friend in a way that reminded me of Miranda Priestly’s own storyline.

Acting & Characters

9.0 / 10
The returning cast still carried the same charm and confidence that made the first movie iconic
The performances felt sharp, funny, and natural, especially from the original characters
The newer characters were interesting but did not get enough room to fully stand out

The acting has not aged a bit since the first movie. Each returning character played their part well, and it was exciting to see the original cast step back into roles that still feel instantly recognizable.

I was also excited to see the main female role from Bridgerton Season 3 appear as the new Emily. That was a fun casting surprise, but I wish the newer characters had more time to show who they were. The movie still followed the original characters so strongly that the new ones were easily overshadowed.

It felt like the writers shaped much of the plot around what fans would want from the old favorites. That worked in some ways, but it also placed the original cast on such a pedestal that the newer additions had less space to breathe.

Visuals & Cinematography

10.0 / 10
The scene transitions and visual flow were beautifully done
The fashion, costume design, and styling felt intentional for each character
The movie never felt visually confusing, and the storytelling was easy to follow

The visuals and cinematography were beautifully done. I never felt confused while watching the movie, and the transitions helped the scenes flow smoothly from one moment to the next.

The costumes were on point. I honestly believe the fashion companies may have had a real hand in designing what fit each specific character. The outfits felt polished, posh, and carefully matched to the personalities on screen.

I also appreciated the homage throughout the film. The styling and design team clearly understood how important fashion is to this franchise. The movie looked beautiful, and that part of the experience absolutely delivered.

Sound & Atmosphere

7.0 / 10
The soundtrack matched the movie’s fashion-forward confidence
Some music choices felt more like direct references to the moment than poetic atmosphere-building
Lady Gaga’s appearance felt too quick and deserved more room in the movie

The sound and atmosphere missed some of what I originally loved from the first movie. In the original, you could hear the busyness of the city, the difference between nightlife and quiet classy moments, and the instrumental touches that made certain scenes feel elegant.

In this movie, the soundtrack still matched the movie’s confidence, but it did not always hit the moment as perfectly. For example, I enjoyed hearing “Vogue” by Madonna during the fashion show, but it felt more like a reference than a perfectly placed emotional or atmospheric choice.

I also loved that they brought in Lady Gaga, but I felt she deserved more of a part. Her moment passed by so quickly that it almost felt abrupt.

Entertainment & Rewatch Value

9.0 / 10
The movie was funny, satisfying, and enjoyable as a fan of the original
Hidden details like cerulean blue and callbacks to older outfits made the experience fun
For non-fans, the rewatch value may be more of a maybe than an absolutely

Although this movie felt like both an homage and a shadow of the original, it was still a great watch for me because I love the first movie. There were so many hidden details, including callbacks to cerulean blue and pieces of fashion history from the original.

As a huge fan, I would absolutely rewatch this movie, but I would want to watch the first movie before it. I do not think I would rewatch the sequel by itself without that original context.

For non-fans, I think the rewatch factor may land closer to “Maybe.” But for me, this is absolutely a rewatch.

Final Verdict

The Devil Wears Prada 2 may not have the same fresh glamour in its story that made the first movie feel so effortless, but it still gave me a wonderful theater experience. It made me laugh, it made me smile, and it reminded me why I enjoyed this world in the first place.

The story could have been stronger, especially with its romance and its attempt to connect back to the original. Still, the acting, fashion, visuals, and fan-service moments made the movie feel worth watching.

I left satisfied. As a fan, I would absolutely watch it again.

Review Criteria

This review is built around five core rating criteria: Story & Writing, Acting & Characters, Visuals & Cinematography, Sound & Atmosphere, and Entertainment & Rewatch Value. Each category represents a different part of the movie experience and is scored independently to keep the review balanced.

Story & Writing looks at the plot, pacing, dialogue, emotional depth, and how well the sequel continues the original story without relying too heavily on nostalgia.

Acting & Characters focuses on performances, chemistry, character development, and whether both returning and new characters feel meaningful.

Visuals & Cinematography evaluates the movie’s visual presentation, scene transitions, camera work, styling, costume design, and overall fashion impact.

Sound & Atmosphere considers music choices, sound design, emotional tone, and how well the audio helps build the world of the movie.

Entertainment & Rewatch Value reflects how enjoyable the movie felt overall and whether I would personally want to watch it again.

The final rating is calculated as an average of all five categories so that each part of the movie contributes equally to the overall score.
