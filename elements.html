Weekend City Walk

A camera, a playlist, and a few hours wandering the city turned into one of my favorite afternoons lately.

I took my camera out with no plan other than to walk. Sometimes those are the best kinds of weekends. No pressure to make it perfect, no strict timeline, just the chance to notice things I would normally miss.

I found little corners of the city that felt cinematic. A bookstore window with warm light. A side street with murals. The sound of traffic blending with music in my headphones. It all felt strangely grounding.

There is something freeing about walking without an agenda. You stop chasing the day and start experiencing it. Every street becomes a possibility. Every turn feels like a small surprise waiting to happen.

I took photos of things that probably looked ordinary to everyone else, but that is the beauty of it. A quiet table by a window. Reflections in glass. Shadows stretching across the sidewalk. The small details ended up feeling like the real story.

By the time I got home, I had a full memory card and a much lighter mind. I think I needed that reminder that beauty can still be found in ordinary places, especially when you are finally moving slow enough to see it.
