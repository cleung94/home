Overview

Designed to support smoother handoffs and better shift organization

This project was created to improve how CNA shift information is captured and carried throughout the workday. During busy shifts, caregivers often need to track multiple responsibilities at once, including patient notes, toileting, vitals, supplies, and shift updates.

I designed this tool to create a more structured and visually intuitive way to manage that information. The layout mirrors the natural flow of a CNA shift, making it easier to quickly record important details, reference them during rounds, and communicate clearly during handoff.

After sharing it with coworkers, it received positive feedback for its usefulness in taking report, giving endorsements, and tracking inventory more clearly across the shift.

Case Study

Problem, goal, and solution

Problem

CNA shifts involve fast task-switching, multiple residents, inventory awareness, vitals, and handoff communication. Important details can easily become scattered across memory, scratch notes, or inconsistent documentation habits.

Goal

Create a structured and easy-to-scan shift helper that supports patient care tracking, quick note-taking, inventory checks, and clearer shift endorsements.

Solution

Designed a color-coded workflow sheet with dedicated sections for assignments, handoff notes, vital signs, inventory, and shift notes to reduce mental load and improve usability during fast-paced CNA work.

Highlights

What this tool helps with

Shift Handoff Clarity

Centralizes report notes and key details so information is easier to carry into the next shift and easier to communicate during endorsements.

Vitals Tracking

Provides dedicated spaces for blood pressure, temperature, pulse, and oxygen so documentation can be captured and reviewed quickly.

Inventory Monitoring

Includes supply tracking for wipes, briefs, pads, gloves, and PPE to help staff identify shortages before they become a problem.

Better Organization

Uses color grouping, boxed sections, and quick-reference structure to make shift information easier to scan during resident rounds.
