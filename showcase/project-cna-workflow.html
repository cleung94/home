Overview

Designed to support smoother understanding of workflow practices

Certified Nursing Assistants manage a wide range of responsibilities during each shift, including patient care tasks, vital sign monitoring, supply awareness, and shift handoff communication. New CNAs often need time to understand the pace of the shift and how responsibilities are typically distributed throughout the day or night.

To support better organization and onboarding, I created a structured workflow helper that visually organizes key tasks and information in a way that mirrors the natural flow of CNA work. The design helps CNAs quickly reference responsibilities, track information during rounds, and communicate more effectively during shift report.

The tool emphasizes simplicity, clear structure, and quick-reference sections so important information can be captured and reviewed easily throughout the shift.

Case Study

Problem, goal, and solution

Problem

CNA shifts require balancing multiple responsibilities simultaneously, including resident care tasks, vital sign collection, supply monitoring, and responding to call lights. For new CNAs, it can be difficult to understand the typical pacing of the shift and how responsibilities are prioritized at different times. Without a structured reference, important information may be tracked inconsistently or rely heavily on memory.

Goal

Create a clear and structured workflow reference that helps CNAs understand the general rhythm of their shift, organize daily responsibilities, and track important information such as vitals, supplies, and resident care tasks.

Solution

I designed a structured shift workflow organizer that visually separates key responsibilities such as assignments, vitals, supply tracking, and shift notes. The layout allows CNAs to quickly record and reference important information throughout the shift. Visual grouping and simple tracking sections make documentation faster and easier during busy periods.

Highlights

What this tool helps with

Structured Shift Workflow

The timeline outlines the typical sequence of responsibilities during PM and NOC shifts, helping CNAs understand the general pacing of the shift and how tasks are commonly distributed across time.

Task Prioritization Guidance

By organizing responsibilities chronologically, the guide helps CNAs recognize which tasks typically occur earlier in the shift versus later rounds, improving task prioritization and time awareness.

Onboarding Support for New CNAs

New CNAs can use the workflow timeline as a reference to better understand expectations during the shift, helping them build confidence and adapt more quickly to the work environment.

Quick Clinical Reference

The guide also includes quick-reference information such as common CNA abbreviations, vital sign ranges, and measurement conversions to support daily clinical tasks.
