Overview

Improving coordination across security infrastructure projects

Throughout the year, the PlanGrid QA inspection process began experiencing communication gaps across multiple teams involved in security infrastructure deployments. Different management approaches and inconsistent expectations led to confusion around project ownership, unclear timelines, and extended review cycles.

In many cases, projects would remain in QA review for extended periods due to incomplete information, delayed communication between integrators and QA teams, or uncertainty about the status of specific tasks.

To address these issues, I redesigned the PlanGrid QA process by introducing a structured workflow timeline, improved project intake procedures, and a centralized tracking system that provided visibility into the status of every active project.

Project Details

  • Role: Process Designer / QA Operations

  • Tools: PlanGrid, Google Forms, Excel Tracking

Case Study

Problem, goal, and solution

Problem

The QA review process for PlanGrid projects lacked a consistent intake process and clearly defined timeline. Integrators, TPMs, and QA analysts often worked on different assumptions regarding project readiness and review ownership. As a result, projects frequently experienced delays, and leadership lacked visibility into where each project stood in the review lifecycle.

Goal

Create a structured QA workflow that clearly defines when a project officially enters QA review, standardize communication between teams, and provide leadership with transparent visibility into project progress, workload distribution, and review timelines.

Solution

I developed a new PlanGrid QA process that introduced a defined project timeline, a formal project intake form, and a centralized tracking system. Integrators now submit projects through a structured Google Form that automatically populates a PlanGrid Tracker, signaling that the project is officially in QA review.

Highlights

Key improvements introduced

Structured QA Timeline

Designed a six-phase workflow that clearly defines the lifecycle of a PlanGrid project from initial submission through final verification and task resolution.

Automated Project Intake

Introduced a Google Form intake process that automatically populates the PlanGrid Tracker, ensuring that projects are formally handed off to QA with complete information.

Centralized Project Tracker

Developed an Excel-based tracking system that monitors project progress, device counts, QA review phases, and the number of open tasks for each project.

Executive Visibility

Configured the tracker to generate weekly reporting updates that provide leadership with clear visibility into project status, workload distribution, and pending tasks.

Impact

Operational improvements

The redesigned PlanGrid process significantly improved cross-team communication and accountability. By clearly defining project intake procedures and establishing a structured QA timeline, teams gained a shared understanding of when projects entered QA review and what steps were required for completion.

The PlanGrid Tracker also provided valuable operational insights, allowing leadership to quickly understand the number of active projects, device counts, QA review stages, and unresolved tasks.

As a result, the average QA review timeline was reduced from approximately three months to one month, improving overall project efficiency and helping teams resolve issues faster.
